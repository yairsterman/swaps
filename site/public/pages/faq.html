How is Swaps different from Airbnb?
Both Swaps and Airbnb present accommodation solutions for travelers based on sharing-economy principles. However, Airbnb and Swaps approach the issue differently, and are geared toward different audiences.

With Airbnb, the process is essentially a monetary transaction similar to booking a hotel room. This means one side sets a price for the value of renting a place, while the other pays for the service. Meanwhile, AirBnb charges both sides for its role in facilitating the transaction. The AirBnb model is best suited for people who are willing to pay a substantial amount for proper accommodation.

With Swaps, on the other hand, you will avoid all the usual expenses that come with accommodation during travel. The transaction between Swappers is not a monetary one, but rather a truly sharing-economy based solution with each side providing the other with a convenient and comfortable home.

The Swaps model assures you don’t blow your budget on accommodation during your trips, and helps you keep your home safe while you travel.

Swap Credit Value System
The value of each Swapper’s home is calculated based on our easy-to-understand Swap Credit Value System.

You use Swap Credits when you stay at another Swapper's home and you gain Swap Credits when a Swapper stays at yours.

The cost per Swap night is easy to understand as well:

1 Swap Credit = 1 Room for 1 Night

In order for the Swap Credit System to work continually and flawlessly, Swaps takes a small commission of 0.5 Swap Credits per night.

In other words, if you’re offering Swappers 3 rooms (gain 2.5 Swap Credits), and you Swap homes with George who is offering 1 room (cost 1 Swap Credit), you will gain a total of 1.5 Swap Credits per night. (2.5 - 1 = 1.5)

On the other hand, if you’re offering Swappers 1 room (gain 0.5 a Swap Credit), and you Swap homes with Sharon who is offering 2 rooms (cost 2 Swap Credits), you will pay a total of 1.5 Swap Credits per night (2 - 0.5 = 1.5)

In one-way Swaps, the host doesn't pay for a Swap since they aren't staying at the other Swapper's home, therefore, the host will only gain credits.

So if you’re offering 2 rooms (gain 1.5 Swap Credits), and Felix stays at your place, you gain a total of 1.5 Swap Credits per night.

Security Deposit
What is the security deposit?
In order to assure our users and their homes are safe during travels, Swaps asks that you set aside a security deposit to cover for possible property damages or cancellations.

In the likely event that no damage was caused to the home when Swapping and no cancellations took place too close to the scheduled Swap date, the entire security deposit is returned to you, without the money ever having to leave your account.
Wait a minute, you said the security deposit doesn’t leave my account, how can that be?
The security deposit is not charged to Swaps, but rather a credit limit is designated to Swaps in your bank or Paypal account.

The security deposit remains in your account and is not charged at all, except in cases where damages were claimed, or if you cancelled a Swap too close to the scheduled Swap date.

Damages and Cancellations
Can I cancel a confirmed Swap?
Yes, you can cancel a confirmed Swap. However, in some cases, you will be required to pay a cancellation fee.
What is a cancellation fee?
Since Swappers who confirmed a Swap are counting on each other, last minute cancellations can cause major inconveniences.

Swaps has set up a cancellation fee, which is used as a compensation that protects Swappers and assures that they are never left stranded in case of an unexpected cancellation.
Under what circumstances will a Swapper have to pay a cancellation fee?
We understand that sometimes there are circumstances that are out of control and that the need to cancel a confirmed Swap my sometimes occur.

For that reason, we allow the cancellation of a confirmed Swap up to one week before the date of the Swap.

However, Since Swappers who confirmed a Swap are counting on each other, last minute cancellations can cause major inconveniences. Therefore, if a Swapper cancelled a Swap less than one week ahead of the confirmed Swap date, Swaps will charge the sum of the security deposit from the canceling Swapper. The funds charged by Swaps will be given as compensation to the second Swapper whose Swap has been canceled through no fault of their own, in order to allow the Swapper to pay for alternative accommodation.
