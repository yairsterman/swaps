How is Swaps different from AirBnb?
While both Swaps and AirBnb present accommodation solutions for travelers based on sharing-economy principles, the two companies approach the issue differently, and are geared toward a different audiences. With AirBnb, the process is essentially a monetary transaction similar to booking a room in a hotel. One side sets a price for the value of renting a place, while the other pays for the service. Meanwhile, AirBnb charges both sides for its role in facilitating the transaction. The AirBnb model is best suited for people who are willing to pay a substantial amount for proper accommodation.

With Swaps, on the other hand, you will avoid all the usual expenses that come with accommodation during travel. The transaction between Swappers is not a monetary one, but rather a truly sharing-economy based solution for accommodation, with each side in the transaction providing the other with a convenient and comfortable home. Swaps requires that Swappers provide a security deposit, which serves as a guarantee covering for very rare cases of damages or cancellations. For the price of a cheap cup of coffee, Swaps acts as a trusted third-party which holds and handles the deposit until after the swap, keeping you safe and secure. As long as no major damages or cancellations occurred, the entire sum of the security deposit will be returned to you in full at the end of your trip.

Setting up a Swap
How do I find a home to Swap?
On the site’s homepage, you can enter a search for homes in specific locations and dates that match your travel plans. You can be flexible if you’d like as well, and broaden your search options.

You can also find the search tab at the top of the site on each page.
Do I need to enter exact dates and travel destinations in order to Swap?
Not at all, you can be flexible when searching for a home. For example, you can choose to search for homes anywhere, or only in cities across Europe, or somewhere in India. Similarly, your dates can be flexible too, and you can search for homes available anytime, or on a specific number of days within a broader date range. The more flexible you are, the better chances you have of finding a great Swap that will be best for you.
Do I need to list my home in order to set up a Swap?
You do not need to list your home in order to search for homes, and are welcome to look around for the perfect spot for you. However, once you have found a home suited for your travel plans, and have attempted to request a Swap, you will be asked to list your home before you can proceed to confirm the Swap. If you have yet to sign up to the site at this stage, Swaps will require you to sign up through Facebook or Google as well.
How do I request a Swap once I’ve found a home that interests me?
Once you’ve reached a Swappers home profile page, you will be able to request a Swap using the button on the right hand side of the page labeled Request a Swap.
Can I contact another Swapper without first requesting a Swap?
Yes, once you’ve reached a Swappers home profile page, you will be able to contact the Swapper using the button on the inner part of the page.
Listing Your Home
How do I list my home on Swaps?
Enter the My Account and go to the My Home tab. Here, you can upload photos and enter information about your home, so that other Swappers can easily find your home and offer to swap with you.

You can enter swap dates and destinations with the Set up Swap label on the site’s upper tab, or in the My Swaps tab in your account.
I’ve listed my home, does this mean any Swapper can find me and request a Swap?
Yes, once you’ve listed your home any Swapper will be able to find you and request a Swap. However, if you would like your home not to show up in searches, or to only show up in searches that match the dates on which you have entered that your home is open to Swap, you can simply uncheck the box labeled “Show my home in all Swap searches” in your account, under the My Home tab.
Is my personal information protected by Swaps?
While most of the information you provided about your home is available for other Swappers to see, your personal information is protected and cannot be accessed by other Swappers. Aside from public information including your first name, your gender, your occupation, and the information you entered in the About Me section in your account, no other personal data can be seen by other Swappers. This means your phone number, email address, and exact home address are not visible to other Swappers if no Swap has been confirmed with them. Once a Swap has been confirmed with another Swapper, your full address will be provided to them, so that they can find the home when they arrive for a Swap. Your email and phone number are used only by us in order to keep in touch with you and to send you updates and confirmations about your Swap, yet they will never be visible to other Swappers.
What should I avoid when messaging another Swapper?
All messages between Swappers are sent only via the Swaps messaging feature, and never through other parties. In order to avoid possible scams and keep yourself safe, never provide credit card information, phone numbers, emails, or other contact information when messaging a fellow Swapper. Remember, Swaps does not require that money be transferred between Swappers, and in no case should someone ask you to give them money. The only time you will be asked for credit card or paypal information is via a protected service once you have requested a Swap.
Security Deposit
What is the security deposit?
In order to protect Swappers from possible damages or unexpected cancellations, each Swapper must provide Swaps with a security deposit ahead of a scheduled home exchange. In the likely event that you caused no damage to the home when swapping and did not cancel your Swap too close to the scheduled Swap date, the entire security deposit is returned to you, without the money ever having to leave your account.
Wait a minute, you said the security deposit doesn’t leave my account, how can that be?
The security deposit is not charged to Swaps, but rather a credit limit is designated to Swaps in your bank or paypal account. The security deposit remains in your account and is not charged at all, except in cases where damages were claimed, or if you cancelled a Swap too close to the scheduled Swap date.
When will I get the deposit back?
In the likely event that you caused no damage to the home when swapping and did not cancel your Swap too close to the scheduled Swap date, the security deposit will be released within 14 days of the end of the Swap, and the entire sum of the security deposit will be returned to you in full.
