The security deposit serves as a guarantee to cover for possible property damages or cancellations. How is Swaps different from Airbnb?
Both Swaps and Airbnb present accommodation solutions for travelers based on sharing-economy principles. However, Airbnb and Swaps approach the issue differently, and are geared toward different audiences.

With Airbnb, the process is essentially a monetary transaction similar to booking a hotel room. This means one side sets a price for the value of renting a place, while the other pays for the service. Meanwhile, AirBnb charges both sides for its role in facilitating the transaction. The AirBnb model is best suited for people who are willing to pay a substantial amount for proper accommodation.

With Swaps, on the other hand, you will avoid all the usual expenses that come with accommodation during travel. The transaction between Swappers is not a monetary one, but rather a truly sharing-economy based solution with each side providing the other with a convenient and comfortable home.

The Swaps model assures you don’t blow your budget on accommodation during your trips, and helps you keep your home safe while you travel.
Setting up a Swap
How do I find a home to Swap?
On the site’s homepage, you can enter a search for homes in specific locations and dates that match your travel plans. You can be flexible if you’d like as well, and broaden your search options.

You can also find the search tab at the top of the site on each page.
Do I need to enter exact dates and travel destinations in order to Swap?
You can be flexible when searching for a home. For example, you can choose to search for homes anywhere, or only in Rome, Italy. Similarly, your date search can be flexible too, and you can search for homes available anytime, or on specific dates, or on a number of days within a broader date range. The more flexible you are, the better chances you have of finding a Swap that suits you.
Do I need to list my home in order to set up a Swap?
You don’t need to list your home in order to search Swaps, and are welcome to look around for the perfect place. However, once you have found a home suited for your travel plans, and have attempted to request a Swap, you will be asked to list your home before you can proceed. If you have yet to sign up to the site at this stage, Swaps will ask that you sign up using Facebook, Gmail, or your email address.
How do I request a Swap once I’ve found a home that interests me?
Once you’ve reached a Swappers home profile page, you will be able to request a Swap using the button on the right hand side of the page labeled Request a Swap.
Can I contact another Swapper without first requesting a Swap?
Yes, once you’ve reached a Swappers home profile page, you will be able to contact the Swapper using the button labeled Send Message on the inner part of the page.
Listing Your Home
How do I list my home on Swaps?
Enter your profile and go to the My Home tab. There, you can upload photos and enter information about your home, so other Swappers can easily find your home and suggest a swap.

You can enter swap dates and destinations using the Set Swap Dates label on the site’s upper tab, or in your profile.
I’ve listed my home, does this mean any Swapper can find me and request a Swap?
Once you’ve listed your home any Swapper will be able to find you and request a Swap. However, if you would like to hide your home from other users’ searches, or to show up only in searches that match your specific dates, simply uncheck the box labeled “Show my home in all Swap searches” in your account, under the My Home tab.
Personal Information and Security
Is my personal information protected by Swaps?
Aside from public information -- which includes your first name, your gender, your occupation, and the information you entered in the About Me section in your profile -- no personal data can be seen or accessed by others. This means your email address and exact home address are not visible to others if no Swap has been confirmed.

Once a Swap has been confirmed with another Swapper, your full address will be provided to them, so that they can find the home when they arrive.

Your email is used only by us in order to keep in touch with you and to send you updates and confirmations about your Swap. They will never be shown to others.
What should I avoid when messaging another Swapper?
All messages between Swappers are sent only via the Swaps messaging feature, and never through other parties. In order to avoid possible scams and keep yourself safe, never provide credit card information, phone numbers, emails, or other contact information when messaging a fellow Swapper. Remember, Swaps does not require that money be transferred between Swappers, and in no case should anyone ask you to transfer them money. The only time you will be asked for credit card or paypal information is via our protected service once you have requested a Swap.
Security Deposit
What is the security deposit?
In order to assure our users and their homes are safe during travels, Swaps asks that you set aside a security deposit to cover for possible property damages or cancellations. In the likely event that no damage was caused to the home when swapping and no cancellations took place too close to the scheduled Swap date, the entire security deposit is returned to you, without the money ever having to leave your account.
Wait a minute, you said the security deposit doesn’t leave my account, how can that be?
The security deposit is not charged to Swaps, but rather a credit limit is designated to Swaps in your bank or paypal account. The security deposit remains in your account and is not charged at all, except in cases where damages were claimed, or if you cancelled a Swap too close to the scheduled Swap date.
When will the credit limit for the security deposit be lifted?
In the likely event that no damage was caused to the home when swapping and no cancellations took place too close to the scheduled Swap date, the credit limit for the security deposit will be lifted within 14 days of the end of the Swap.
How much money do I have to set aside for the security deposit?
Swaps offers 3 different security deposit plans, each suited for a different kind of Swapper.
  • Nomad - 70$ deposit per week. This plan covers you for up to 150$ in damages or cancellations (This plan costs 5$ per night).
  • Explorer - 350$ deposit per week. This plan covers you for up to 750$ in damages or cancellations (This plan costs 14$ per night)
  • Cosmopolite - 1,000$ deposit per week. This plan covers you for up to 2,000$ in damages or cancellations (This plan costs 29$ per night)
Remember, your first Swap deal (regardless of its length) is on us! You’ll still have to set aside a security deposit, but it will be returned to you in full after the Swap, and the Swap deal itself is free.
Who receives the security deposit?
The security deposit is not charged to Swaps, but rather a credit limit is designated to Swaps in your bank or paypal account. This means that the security deposit remains in your account and is not charged at all, except in cases where damages were claimed, or if you cancelled a Swap too close to the scheduled Swap date.
Are there any cases where my security deposit will be charged by Swaps?
Yes, Swaps charges some or all of the security deposit in two rare cases -- if damage claims were filed by the Swapper with which you swapped homes, and if you cancelled a Swap too close to a scheduled Swap date.
Damages and Cancellations
Can I cancel a confirmed swap?
Yes, you can cancel a confirmed Swap. However, in some cases, you will be required to pay a cancellation fee.
What is a cancellation fee?
Since Swappers who confirmed a swap are counting on each other, last minute cancellations can cause major inconveniences. Swaps has set up a cancellation fee, which is used as a compensation that protects Swappers and assures that they are never left stranded in case of an unexpected cancellation.
Under what circumstances will a Swapper have to pay a cancellation fee?
We understand that sometimes there are circumstances that are out of control and that the need to cancel a confirmed Swap my sometimes occur. For that reason, we allow the cancellation of a confirmed Swap up to one week before the date of the Swap. However, Since Swappers who confirmed a Swap are counting on each other, last minute cancellations can cause major inconveniences. Therefore, if a Swapper cancelled a Swap less than one week ahead of the confirmed Swap date, Swaps will charge the sum of the security deposit from the canceling Swapper. The funds charged by Swaps will be given as compensation to the second Swapper whose swap had been canceled through no fault of his own, in order to allow the Swapper to pay for alternative accommodation.
